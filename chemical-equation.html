Chemical Equation

Short Definition

A chemical equation is a symbolic representation of a chemical reaction using chemical formulas.

Detailed Explanation

A chemical equation describes a chemical reaction in a clear and simple form. In a chemical equation, the substances that react are called reactants and the substances that are produced are called products.

Reactants are written on the left side of the equation while products are written on the right side. An arrow is used between them to show the direction of the reaction.

For example, when hydrogen reacts with oxygen it forms water. This reaction can be written as a chemical equation. Chemical equations help scientists understand how substances change during reactions.

Chemical equations must be balanced. This means the number of atoms of each element must be the same on both sides of the equation. Balancing equations follows the law of conservation of mass.
