Physical Change

Short Definition

A physical change is a change in the form or appearance of a substance without changing its chemical composition.

Detailed Explanation

A physical change occurs when the physical properties of a substance change but the substance itself remains the same. Examples include cutting, melting, freezing, and boiling.

During a physical change, no new substance is formed. Only the shape, size, or state of the substance changes.

For example, when ice melts into water it is still the same substance. The chemical composition of water does not change.

Physical changes are usually reversible. This means the substance can often return to its original form.
