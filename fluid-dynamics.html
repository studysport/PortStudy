Fluid Dynamics

Short Definition:

Fluid dynamics is the study of fluids (liquids and gases) in motion.

Detailed Explanation:

Fluids can flow and take the shape of their container. Important concepts in fluid dynamics include pressure, density, viscosity, and flow rate.

Bernoulli’s principle states that when the speed of a fluid increases, its pressure decreases. This principle explains airplane lift and fluid flow in pipes.

The equation of continuity explains that the mass of fluid flowing through a pipe remains constant.

Key Points:

  • Fluids include liquids and gases
  • Pressure and velocity are related
  • Bernoulli’s principle is important
