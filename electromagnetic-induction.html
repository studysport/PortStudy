Electromagnetic Induction

Short Definition:

Electromagnetic induction is the production of induced current due to change in magnetic flux.

Detailed Explanation:

Faraday discovered that when magnetic flux linked with a coil changes, an electromotive force (EMF) is induced.

Faraday’s Law states that induced EMF is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux.

Lenz’s Law gives the direction of induced current. It states that induced current always opposes the change producing it.

Generators work on the principle of electromagnetic induction.

Key Points:

  • Change in magnetic flux induces EMF
  • Faraday’s Law explains induction
  • Lenz’s Law gives direction
  • Generators use this principle
