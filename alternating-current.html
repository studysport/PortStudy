Alternating Current

Short Definition:

Alternating current (AC) is the electric current that changes direction periodically.

Detailed Explanation:

Unlike direct current (DC), alternating current reverses its direction after equal intervals of time. The frequency of AC is measured in hertz (Hz).

The peak value and RMS value are important in AC analysis. Transformers are used to increase or decrease AC voltage.

AC is preferred for long-distance transmission because it reduces power loss.

Key Points:

  • AC changes direction periodically
  • Measured in hertz
  • Transformers work on AC
