Work and Energy

Short Definition:

Work is done when a force causes displacement, and energy is the capacity to do work.

Detailed Explanation:

Work is defined as the product of force and displacement in the direction of the force. Mathematically, W = F × d × cosθ.

Energy exists in various forms such as kinetic energy (energy of motion) and potential energy (stored energy). Kinetic energy is given by KE = ½mv², and potential energy is given by PE = mgh.

The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed; it only changes from one form to another.

Power is the rate at which work is done and is measured in watts.

Key Points:

  • Work depends on force and displacement
  • Energy has many forms
  • Energy is conserved
  • Power is rate of doing work
