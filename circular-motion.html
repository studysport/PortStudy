Circular Motion

Short Definition:

Circular motion is the motion of an object along a circular path.

Detailed Explanation:

In circular motion, the direction of velocity changes continuously even if speed remains constant. This change in direction means acceleration is present.

The force responsible for circular motion is called centripetal force, which acts toward the center of the circle. It is given by Fc = mv²/r.

Examples of circular motion include the motion of planets around the sun and a stone tied to a string being rotated.

Key Points:

  • Direction of velocity changes
  • Centripetal force acts inward
  • Acceleration exists even at constant speed
