Covalent Bond

Short Definition

A covalent bond is a chemical bond formed when atoms share electrons with each other.

Detailed Explanation

A covalent bond is formed when two atoms share one or more pairs of electrons. This type of bonding usually occurs between non-metal atoms. By sharing electrons, the atoms achieve a stable electron configuration in their outer shell.

Covalent bonds form molecules. For example, in a water molecule two hydrogen atoms share electrons with one oxygen atom. This sharing of electrons creates stable chemical bonds that hold the atoms together.

Covalent compounds usually have lower melting and boiling points compared to ionic compounds. Many covalent compounds exist as gases or liquids at room temperature.

Covalent bonds are extremely important in organic chemistry because most biological molecules such as proteins, carbohydrates, and DNA are formed through covalent bonding.
