Electrolysis

Short Definition

Electrolysis is the process of using electrical energy to cause a chemical reaction.

Detailed Explanation

Electrolysis is a chemical process in which an electric current passes through a liquid or molten compound and causes it to break down into simpler substances. This process occurs in substances that contain free ions which can move and carry electric charge.

During electrolysis, two electrodes are placed in the liquid or molten compound. One electrode is called the anode and the other is called the cathode. Positive ions move toward the cathode while negative ions move toward the anode.

When these ions reach the electrodes, chemical reactions occur that produce new substances. For example, electrolysis of water produces hydrogen gas and oxygen gas.

Electrolysis is widely used in industries for extracting metals, electroplating objects with metals, and producing important chemicals such as chlorine and hydrogen.
