Immunity

Short Definition

Immunity is the body's natural ability to defend itself against infections and harmful microorganisms.

Detailed Explanation

The immune system is a complex network of cells, tissues, and organs that protect the body from pathogens such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites. When harmful microorganisms enter the body, the immune system identifies them as foreign and attacks them.

White blood cells play a major role in immunity. They produce antibodies which recognize and destroy harmful pathogens. The immune system also has memory cells that remember previous infections and respond more quickly if the same pathogen attacks again.

There are two main types of immunity: innate immunity and acquired immunity. Innate immunity is the natural protection present at birth, while acquired immunity develops after exposure to pathogens or through vaccination.
