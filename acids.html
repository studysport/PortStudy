Acids

Short Definition

Acids are substances that release hydrogen ions when dissolved in water.

Detailed Explanation

Acids are important chemical substances that have a sour taste and can react with metals and bases. Examples of common acids include hydrochloric acid, sulfuric acid, and acetic acid.

Acids turn blue litmus paper red and have a pH value less than 7. They are widely used in industries, laboratories, and even in the human stomach where hydrochloric acid helps in digestion.

Strong acids can be dangerous and must be handled carefully because they can damage skin and materials.
