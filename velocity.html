Velocity

Short Definition

Velocity is the speed of an object in a particular direction.

Detailed Explanation

Velocity describes how fast an object moves and in which direction it moves. Unlike speed, velocity includes direction as an important factor. For example, if a car moves at 60 km/h toward the north, its velocity includes both speed and direction.

Velocity can change if either the speed or direction of an object changes. When the velocity of an object changes, it means the object is accelerating.

Velocity helps scientists understand the motion of objects such as vehicles, projectiles, and planets moving in space.

Key Points

  • Velocity includes both speed and direction.
  • Change in velocity means acceleration occurs.
  • Velocity is important for studying motion.
