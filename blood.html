Blood

Short Definition

Blood is a red liquid connective tissue that circulates in the human body and transports oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste materials.

Detailed Explanation

Blood is one of the most important fluids in the human body. It flows through a network of blood vessels such as arteries, veins, and capillaries. The main function of blood is to transport oxygen from the lungs to all parts of the body and bring carbon dioxide back to the lungs for removal.

Blood is composed of four main components: red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, and plasma. Red blood cells carry oxygen with the help of a protein called hemoglobin. White blood cells protect the body from infections and diseases. Platelets help in blood clotting which prevents excessive bleeding when the body is injured. Plasma is the liquid part of blood that carries nutrients, hormones, and waste materials.

Blood also helps regulate body temperature, maintain pH balance, and support the immune system. Without blood circulation, the body cannot survive because cells will not receive oxygen and nutrients.
