Acceleration

Short Definition

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with respect to time.

Detailed Explanation

Acceleration occurs when the velocity of an object changes over time. This change may involve an increase in speed, a decrease in speed, or a change in direction. For example, when a car speeds up, slows down, or turns, acceleration occurs.

Acceleration plays an important role in understanding motion. Even if an object moves at constant speed but changes direction, it still experiences acceleration. A common example is circular motion, where the direction of motion constantly changes.

Scientists study acceleration to understand how objects respond to forces and how motion changes under different conditions.

Key Points

  • Acceleration is the change in velocity over time.
  • It occurs when speed or direction changes.
  • Acceleration helps explain many types of motion.
