Mixture

Short Definition

A mixture is a combination of two or more substances that are physically mixed but not chemically combined.

Detailed Explanation

A mixture is formed when different substances are combined together without undergoing a chemical reaction. Each substance in the mixture keeps its original properties.

Mixtures can be separated by physical methods such as filtration, evaporation, and distillation. For example, sand and salt mixed together can be separated easily.

There are two main types of mixtures: homogeneous mixtures and heterogeneous mixtures. Homogeneous mixtures have a uniform composition while heterogeneous mixtures have different visible parts.

Examples of mixtures include air, salt water, and soil. Mixtures are very common in daily life and are important in many chemical processes.
