Circulatory System

Short Definition

The circulatory system transports blood, oxygen, and nutrients throughout the body.

Detailed Explanation

The circulatory system is responsible for moving blood throughout the body. It delivers oxygen and nutrients to cells and removes carbon dioxide and waste products.

The heart acts as a pump that pushes blood through blood vessels called arteries, veins, and capillaries. Arteries carry blood away from the heart, veins carry blood back to the heart, and capillaries allow exchange of substances between blood and body cells.

This system works continuously to maintain proper functioning of organs and tissues.

Key Points

  • The heart pumps blood throughout the body.
  • Blood vessels carry blood to and from organs.
  • The circulatory system transports oxygen and nutrients.
