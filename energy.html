Energy

Short Definition

Energy is the ability to do work.

Detailed Explanation

Energy is a fundamental concept in physics and is defined as the ability to perform work. Energy exists in many forms such as mechanical energy, thermal energy, electrical energy, chemical energy, and nuclear energy.

Mechanical energy is divided into two main types: kinetic energy and potential energy. Kinetic energy is the energy of motion, while potential energy is stored energy due to position or condition.

Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only change from one form to another. This principle is known as the law of conservation of energy. For example, electrical energy can be converted into light and heat in a bulb.

Key Points

  • Energy is the ability to do work.
  • It exists in many forms.
  • Energy can change from one form to another.
