Motion and Force

Short Definition:

Motion is the change in position of an object with respect to time, and force is a push or pull that can change motion.

Detailed Explanation:

Motion describes how an object changes its position over time. It can be described using quantities such as distance, displacement, velocity, and acceleration.

Force is responsible for changing the state of motion of an object. According to Newton’s First Law, a body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Newton’s Second Law states that force equals mass times acceleration (F = ma). This law explains how the motion of an object changes when force is applied.

Newton’s Third Law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. These laws form the foundation of classical mechanics.

Key Points:

  • Motion involves displacement and velocity
  • Force causes acceleration
  • Newton’s laws explain motion
