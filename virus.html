Virus

Short Definition

A virus is a tiny infectious agent that can reproduce only inside the cells of a living organism.

Detailed Explanation

Viruses are extremely small particles that are even smaller than bacteria. They cannot carry out life processes on their own and therefore need a host cell to survive and reproduce.

When a virus enters a living cell, it uses the cell's machinery to make copies of itself. These new viruses then spread to other cells and continue the infection.

Viruses cause many diseases in humans, animals, and plants. Examples include influenza, COVID-19, measles, and AIDS. Vaccines are often used to protect people from viral infections.
