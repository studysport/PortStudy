Cell

Short Definition

A cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life.

Detailed Explanation

A cell is the smallest unit of life that can perform all the necessary functions required for living organisms. Every living organism is made up of one or more cells. Some organisms consist of a single cell and are called unicellular organisms, while others are made of many cells and are called multicellular organisms.

Cells contain different structures called organelles that perform specific functions. For example, the nucleus controls the activities of the cell, mitochondria produce energy, and the cell membrane controls what enters and leaves the cell.

Cells are important because they carry out all the processes necessary for life such as growth, reproduction, metabolism, and response to the environment.

Key Points

  • The cell is the basic unit of life.
  • All living organisms are made of cells.
  • Cells contain organelles that perform specific functions.
