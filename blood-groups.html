Blood Groups

Short Definition

Blood groups are classifications of blood based on the presence or absence of specific antigens on the surface of red blood cells.

Detailed Explanation

Human blood is divided into different groups depending on the type of antigens present on the surface of red blood cells. The most common blood group system is the ABO system which includes four main groups: A, B, AB, and O.

People with blood group A have A antigens, those with group B have B antigens, group AB has both antigens, and group O has none. Blood group O is known as the universal donor, while blood group AB is known as the universal recipient.

Another important factor in blood classification is the Rh factor. If Rh protein is present, the blood group is Rh positive; if absent, it is Rh negative. Blood grouping is very important during blood transfusion because incompatible blood can cause serious medical reactions and may even be life-threatening.
