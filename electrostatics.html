Electrostatics

Short Definition:

Electrostatics is the branch of physics that deals with electric charges at rest and the forces between them.

Detailed Explanation:

Electrostatics studies the behavior of stationary electric charges. There are two types of electric charges: positive and negative. Like charges repel each other, while unlike charges attract each other.

Coulomb’s Law explains the force between two point charges. It states that the force is directly proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. Mathematically:

F = k q₁q₂ / r²

Electric field is defined as the force experienced per unit positive charge. Electric potential is the work done in bringing a unit positive charge from infinity to a point.

Capacitors are devices used to store electric charge. The capacitance depends on the area of plates, distance between them, and dielectric material.

Key Points:

  • Deals with stationary charges
  • Coulomb’s Law explains electric force
  • Electric field and potential are important concepts
  • Capacitors store electric energy
