Hormones

Short Definition

Hormones are chemical messengers produced by endocrine glands that regulate many important functions of the body.

Detailed Explanation

Hormones are secreted by specialized glands known as endocrine glands. These glands release hormones directly into the bloodstream. Once released, hormones travel through the blood to target organs where they control different biological processes.

Hormones regulate many important body activities such as growth, metabolism, mood, reproduction, and development. For example, the thyroid gland produces hormones that control metabolism, while the pancreas releases insulin which regulates blood sugar levels.

Other important hormones include adrenaline, which prepares the body for emergency situations, and growth hormone which controls body development during childhood. Hormones maintain balance in the body and ensure that organs function properly.
