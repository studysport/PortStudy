Electromagnetism

Short Definition:

Electromagnetism is the study of magnetic effects produced by electric current.

Detailed Explanation:

When electric current flows through a conductor, it produces a magnetic field around it. This phenomenon was discovered by Oersted.

The direction of magnetic field around a current-carrying conductor can be determined by the right-hand rule.

A moving charge experiences a magnetic force when placed in a magnetic field. Electric motors work on this principle.

Magnetic flux and magnetic field strength are important concepts in electromagnetism.

Key Points:

  • Current produces magnetic field
  • Right-hand rule determines direction
  • Electric motors use this principle
