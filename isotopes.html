Isotopes

Short Definition

Isotopes are atoms of the same element that have the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons.

Detailed Explanation

Isotopes are different forms of the same element. They have the same atomic number because they contain the same number of protons in the nucleus. However, they differ in their atomic mass because the number of neutrons is different.

For example, carbon has several isotopes such as carbon-12, carbon-13, and carbon-14. All of these isotopes contain six protons but the number of neutrons varies.

Some isotopes are stable, which means they do not change over time. Other isotopes are radioactive and release energy in the form of radiation. These radioactive isotopes are used in medical treatments, scientific research, and nuclear energy production.

Isotopes are also used in carbon dating to determine the age of ancient objects and fossils. Because of their importance, isotopes play a major role in modern science and technology.
