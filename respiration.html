Respiration

Short Definition

Respiration is the process by which living organisms release energy from food.

Detailed Explanation

Respiration is a biological process that occurs in all living organisms. During respiration, food molecules such as glucose are broken down to release energy needed for various life activities such as movement, growth, and reproduction.

Respiration mainly occurs in the mitochondria of cells. In aerobic respiration, oxygen is used to break down glucose and produce energy, carbon dioxide, and water. In anaerobic respiration, energy is produced without the use of oxygen.

Respiration is essential for survival because it provides the energy required for all biological processes in living organisms.

Key Points

  • Respiration releases energy from food.
  • It occurs in the cells of living organisms.
  • Energy produced is used for life processes.
