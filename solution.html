Solution

Short Definition

A solution is a homogeneous mixture in which one substance dissolves in another substance.

Detailed Explanation

A solution is formed when one substance called the solute dissolves in another substance called the solvent. The particles of the solute spread evenly throughout the solvent, creating a uniform mixture.

One common example of a solution is salt dissolved in water. In this case, salt acts as the solute and water acts as the solvent. The salt particles break into very small ions that mix evenly with the water molecules.

Solutions are very important in chemistry and daily life. Many beverages, medicines, and chemical mixtures are solutions. Solutions can exist in different states such as solid solutions, liquid solutions, and gaseous solutions.

The concentration of a solution describes how much solute is dissolved in the solvent. Concentrated solutions contain a large amount of solute, while dilute solutions contain a small amount of solute.
