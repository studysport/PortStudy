pH Scale

Short Definition

The pH scale is used to measure how acidic or basic a substance is.

Detailed Explanation

The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14. A pH value of 7 is considered neutral, which means the substance is neither acidic nor basic.

Substances with a pH less than 7 are acidic, while substances with a pH greater than 7 are basic or alkaline. For example, lemon juice and vinegar are acidic, while soap and baking soda are basic.

The pH scale is very important in chemistry, biology, agriculture, and environmental science because many chemical processes depend on the correct pH level.
