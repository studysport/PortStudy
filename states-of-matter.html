States of Matter

Short Definition

States of matter are the different forms in which matter exists such as solid, liquid, and gas.

Detailed Explanation

Matter exists in different physical forms known as states of matter. The three common states are solid, liquid, and gas.

In solids, particles are very closely packed and vibrate in fixed positions. Solids have a definite shape and volume.

In liquids, particles are less tightly packed and can move around each other. Liquids have a definite volume but do not have a fixed shape. They take the shape of their container.

In gases, particles move very freely and are far apart from each other. Gases do not have a fixed shape or volume and can expand to fill any container.

Changes between these states occur when heat energy is added or removed from a substance.
