Chemical Change

Short Definition

A chemical change is a process in which one or more substances are transformed into new substances.

Detailed Explanation

A chemical change occurs when substances react with each other and form new substances with different properties. This process involves breaking and forming chemical bonds.

Examples of chemical changes include burning wood, rusting iron, and cooking food. In these processes, the original substances change into new materials.

Chemical changes are usually difficult to reverse. Once the reaction has occurred, it is not easy to return to the original substances.

Signs of a chemical change include the production of heat, light, gas, or a color change during the reaction.
