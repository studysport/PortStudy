Force

Short Definition

Force is a push or pull that can change the motion or shape of an object.

Detailed Explanation

Force is one of the fundamental concepts in physics. It is defined as a push or pull applied to an object. When a force acts on an object, it can change the object's position, speed, direction, or shape. For example, pushing a door to open it, kicking a football, or pulling a rope are all examples of force.

Forces can act through direct contact or from a distance. Contact forces include pushing, pulling, friction, and tension. Non-contact forces act without physical contact, such as gravitational force, magnetic force, and electric force.

In everyday life, many forces act on objects around us. Gravity pulls objects toward the Earth, friction slows down moving objects, and applied force helps move objects from one place to another.

Key Points

  • Force is a push or pull.
  • It can change motion, direction, or shape.
  • Forces can be contact or non-contact.
