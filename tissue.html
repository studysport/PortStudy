Tissue

Short Definition

A tissue is a group of similar cells that work together to perform a specific function.

Detailed Explanation

Tissues are formed when similar types of cells come together to perform a specific function in the body. In multicellular organisms, tissues are essential because they help organize the body's structure and functions.

In animals, there are four main types of tissues: epithelial tissue, connective tissue, muscle tissue, and nervous tissue. Each type performs different roles in the body such as protection, support, movement, and communication.

In plants, tissues are mainly divided into meristematic tissues and permanent tissues. These tissues help plants grow and perform different functions such as transport of water and nutrients.

Key Points

  • Tissues are groups of similar cells.
  • They perform specific functions in organisms.
  • Animal and plant tissues have different types.
