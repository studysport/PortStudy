Photosynthesis

Short Definition

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food using sunlight.

Detailed Explanation

Photosynthesis is a vital biological process that occurs in green plants, algae, and some bacteria. During this process, plants use sunlight, carbon dioxide from the air, and water from the soil to produce food in the form of glucose.

This process takes place mainly in the leaves of plants inside structures called chloroplasts. Chlorophyll, the green pigment in plants, absorbs sunlight and provides the energy needed for photosynthesis.

Photosynthesis is very important because it provides food for plants and releases oxygen into the atmosphere. This oxygen is essential for the survival of most living organisms on Earth.

Key Points

  • Plants use sunlight to make food.
  • Chlorophyll absorbs light energy.
  • Photosynthesis produces oxygen.
