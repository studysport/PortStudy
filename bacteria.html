Bacteria

Short Definition

Bacteria are microscopic single-celled organisms that exist in almost every environment on Earth.

Detailed Explanation

Bacteria are extremely small living organisms that can only be seen with the help of a microscope. They are among the oldest forms of life on Earth and can survive in many environments such as soil, water, air, and inside living organisms.

Some bacteria are beneficial to humans. For example, certain bacteria help in digestion in the human intestine. Others are used in food production such as yogurt, cheese, and fermented foods.

However, some bacteria are harmful and cause diseases such as tuberculosis, cholera, and food poisoning. Scientists study bacteria to develop antibiotics that can treat bacterial infections.
