Current Electricity

Short Definition:

Current electricity is the study of electric charges in motion.

Detailed Explanation:

Electric current is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor. It is measured in amperes (A). The flow of current requires a potential difference and a closed circuit.

Ohm’s Law states that current is directly proportional to voltage and inversely proportional to resistance:

V = IR

Resistance depends on length, area, and material of the conductor. Combination of resistors can be in series or parallel.

Electrical power is the rate of energy consumption and is given by:

P = VI

Key Points:

  • Electric current is flow of charge
  • Ohm’s Law: V = IR
  • Resistors can be series or parallel
  • Power formula P = VI
