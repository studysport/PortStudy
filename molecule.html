Molecule

Short Definition

A molecule is a group of two or more atoms chemically bonded together.

Detailed Explanation

Molecules are formed when atoms combine with each other through chemical bonds. These bonds hold the atoms together and allow them to form stable structures.

Molecules can consist of atoms of the same element or different elements. For example, an oxygen molecule contains two oxygen atoms, while a water molecule contains two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.

Molecules play a very important role in chemistry because they form the substances we see and use in daily life. The properties of substances depend on the types of atoms and how they are arranged in the molecule.
