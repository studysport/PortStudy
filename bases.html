Bases

Short Definition

Bases are substances that release hydroxide ions when dissolved in water.

Detailed Explanation

Bases are chemical substances that often have a bitter taste and a slippery feel. They are the opposite of acids in many chemical reactions.

Examples of bases include sodium hydroxide, potassium hydroxide, and ammonia. Bases turn red litmus paper blue and usually have a pH greater than 7.

Bases are commonly used in soap making, cleaning products, and many industrial processes.
