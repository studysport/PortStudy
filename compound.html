Compound

Short Definition

A compound is a substance formed when two or more different elements chemically combine in a fixed ratio.

Detailed Explanation

Compounds are created through chemical reactions where atoms of different elements bond together. These bonds form stable molecules with unique properties.

For example, water is a compound made of hydrogen and oxygen atoms. Salt is another compound formed from sodium and chlorine.

The properties of a compound are usually very different from the properties of the elements that form it. Compounds can only be separated into their elements by chemical reactions.
